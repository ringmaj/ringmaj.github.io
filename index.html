Hello!

My name is Majok Ring, I am a 4th year undergraduate student at the University of California Merced. I am in the Computer Science and Engineering program and will graduate in May 2018.

During my studies at UC Merced, I completed essential courses such as Database Systems, Introduction to Artificial Intelligence, Object Oriented Programming, Computer Architecture, Computer Graphics. These courses have created a foundation for most of my programming have helped me develop essential skills which I apply to research projects, hackathons, and mobile app challenges. With these experiences, I learned the many processes of creating a program and working in a team environment to achieve a goal.

Coursework: For the Fall 2017 semester, I will be taking Computer Networks (CSE 160), Computers and Networks Security (CSE 178), Circuit Theory (ENGR 65), and Professional Seminar (ENGR 191). I am very interested in Computer Networking and as you can see by my upcoming courses, am preparing myself for a foundation in this area. During the summer, I am reading and learning more about this subject to hopefully join a lab on campus during the Fall/Spring semester.

Teamwork: Throughout my years, I have proven myself very skilled at working in interdisciplinary teams and communicating well. From my experience working on TEDxYouth@SanDiego, I worked with students and mentors to develop a TED talks program for students within the San Diego area. On the partnership team, I worked with 4 other students and mentors to raise $60,000 in funds for our event over the course of 8 months. Through weekly meetings and email updates, I learned to communicate with large local companies such as Life Technologies, PIRCH, Sodexo, DPR, SDG&E, Westreich Foundation, and the UC San Diego Extension.

Undergraduate Research/Involvement: During my first year at university, I sought out organizations to further my understanding on Big Data analytics and gain experience. I ended up finding a graduate student who shared the same values and I helped found our organization, The Quantitative Project, in 2014. Through my time as a Secretary and other leadership positions, I have guided students to foster investigative research and lead independent projects to gain necessary experience for the future. I worked on projects such as an RNA Sequencing Analysis Web Pipeline (IP), MakerCorner, Programming Bootcamps, and BiotaQ.

As exemplified by the above, I have a strong interest in software development and have the discipline and communication skills to be successful in a team environment. I am confident that I will be a very functional addition to your team of engineers.
